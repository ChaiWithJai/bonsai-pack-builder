# PrismML Proof Timeline

Research timeline for substantiating the narrative:

> PrismML emerged from a team of Caltech researchers and was founded with support from Khosla Ventures, Cerberus, and Google. The team spent years tackling one of the field's hard problems: compressing neural networks without sacrificing reasoning ability.

This is a proof map, not a press release. Each item is labeled as:

- **Proved**: directly supported by a public source.
- **Inferred**: a reasonable synthesis across sources, but not stated as-is by PrismML.
- **Needs confirmation**: useful for the pitch, but should be confirmed with the team before outward use.

Last updated: 2026-06-27.

## Executive Read

The public evidence supports a founder arc with three layers:

1. **Research depth**: Babak Hassibi's Caltech research record spans information theory, signal processing, control, machine learning, high-dimensional statistics, random matrices, fast linear algebra, and neural networks. That is the technical lineage behind the "years of mathematical theory" claim. See [Caltech EE profile](https://www.ee.caltech.edu/people/hassibi).
2. **Translation into adoption**: Reza Sadri's Caltech AI Bootcamp work proves a separate, important muscle: teaching researchers when AI is useful, when it is not, and how to map real problems into ML workflows. See [Caltech Magazine](https://magazine.caltech.edu/post/reza-sadri-ai-bootcamp) and [ENGenuity one-year report](https://engenuity.caltech.edu/stories/EAS-AI-Bootcamp-Progress-Report-One-Year-In).
3. **Infrastructure-market validation**: Khosla frames the investment around intelligence per unit of energy/cost, Cerberus brings edge/AI/silicon investment logic through Amir Salek, and Google is publicly tied to the model training path via Google v4 TPUs. See [PrismML launch news](https://prismml.com/news/prismml-launches-worlds-first-1-bit-ai-model), [Cerberus Amir Salek profile](https://www.cerberus.com/our-firm/leadership/amir-salek/), and [Google Cloud TPU docs](https://cloud.google.com/tpu).

The safest one-line:

> PrismML's public proof stack is Caltech mathematical depth, a 1-bit Bonsai release benchmarked for edge intelligence density, and strategic validation from investors/operators who care about the economics of inference.

## Means, Motivation, Opportunity

### Means

What the public proof says PrismML can do now:

- Release open Bonsai models in 8B, 4B, and 1.7B sizes.
- Demonstrate a model-size / throughput / energy-efficiency claim against full-precision peers.
- Train at serious scale using Google v4 TPUs.
- Publish a benchmark framing around "intelligence density."

What our pack-builder should add:

- A harness that turns local-model density into a business workflow.
- A benchmark template that records device, model, latency, gate result, and claim limits.
- A repeatable path from demo to contribution.

### Motivation

PrismML's motivation is not "smaller for smaller's sake." Public messaging repeatedly points to:

- local execution,
- privacy-sensitive workflows,
- lower latency,
- lower energy,
- fewer datacenter constraints,
- and AI on consumer/industrial edge hardware.

Our use case should therefore avoid generic "agent builder" language and focus on a job where local execution matters: regulated notes, clean egress, human approval, and benchmarkable limits.

### Opportunity

The opportunity is to become the reference harness for:

- healthcare and regulated-workflow demos;
- developer contributions to PrismML's benchmark templates;
- education around "model as component, verifier as boundary";
- and public proof that Bonsai creates adoptable value, not only a model leaderboard result.

## Suggested Use In The PrismML Briefcase

> The reason this lands is that PrismML's public record already contains both halves of the story. Babak and the research team prove the math: years of compression work turned into Bonsai, a 1-bit model family for local intelligence. Reza's Caltech AI Bootcamp proves the adoption muscle: teach smart people how to map real problems into AI workflows and where not to use AI. Khosla and Cerberus validate the economics. Our pack-builder turns those proof points into a reference workflow: a regulated job, a local model, a verifier gate, synthetic evals, and a contribution path back into the benchmark ecosystem.
